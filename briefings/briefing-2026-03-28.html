ChatGPT Ads Expand to Canada — OpenAI Grows Ad Business to 600+ Advertisers

OpenAI is bringing its ChatGPT ad pilot to Canada, Australia, and New Zealand in the coming weeks after U.S. results showed "no impact on consumer trust metrics" and over $100 million in annualized ad revenue within just six weeks of launch. For Canadian digital agencies, this is a pivotal moment: ChatGPT now competes directly with Google and Meta as an ad placement channel, and major holding companies WPP, Omnicom, and Dentsu are already in the testing program. The model targets ads to the topic of a user's current conversation, past chats, and ad interaction history — a new kind of contextual intent signal that's fundamentally different from keyword or behavioural targeting. Agencies should begin scoping ChatGPT ad testing budgets now; Truist projects OpenAI ad revenue will surpass $30 billion by 2030, making this the ground floor of a major new media channel.

Bloomberg 3 min read Happening Now

Apple Maps Paid Ads Launch in US & Canada This Summer

Apple confirmed it will launch search ads in Apple Maps in the United States and Canada this summer — a direct challenge to Google Maps' local advertising dominance. Ads will surface at the top of Maps search results and in a new "Suggested Places" experience, using on-device processing so user location data never leaves the device and is never shared with advertisers. This matters enormously for paid media strategy: Apple Maps is installed on 2.5 billion active devices, and Apple's privacy-first ad model could attract advertisers who have retreated from Google's data-heavy ecosystem. As part of the broader Apple Business platform launching April 14, campaign setup promises to be self-serve and accessible — expect rapid adoption, especially from Canadian multi-location retailers, restaurants, and service businesses.

Search Engine Land 3 min read

Google Drops Its First Core Update of 2026 — SEMrush Volatility Hits 9.5/10

Google began rolling out the March 2026 Core Update on Friday March 27, its first broad core update of the year, with a rollout window of up to two weeks. Early data from SEMrush shows volatility scores peaking at 9.5 out of 10 — one of the highest ever recorded — with over 55% of monitored sites experiencing ranking shifts and some reporting organic traffic drops of 20–35%. This update is believed to leverage Google's Gemini 4.0 Semantic Filter to penalize AI-generated content produced at scale without meaningful editorial oversight, while rewarding pages with proprietary data, original research, and genuine E-E-A-T signals. For agencies managing organic content strategies, audit any client sites that rely heavily on AI-generated blog content now; recoveries are possible but typically take at least one additional update cycle.

Ad Age 3 min read

Omnicom–IPG Merger Complete: The Ad Industry's "Big Two" Era Has Arrived

The Omnicom–IPG merger officially closed in November 2025 with ~$26 billion in combined revenue, displacing WPP as the world's largest ad holding company and effectively reducing the old Big Six to a "Big Two" alongside Publicis. The merged entity consolidated creative networks down to three global shops (BBDO, McCann, TBWA), created a media super-group with six flagship networks, and cut an estimated 4,000 jobs worldwide. The strategic crown jewel is IPG's Acxiom — a $2 billion consumer identity platform now powering Omnicom's AI and first-party data strategy. For independent agencies like NP Digital, this consolidation creates a dual opportunity: pitch against the bureaucratic complexity of mega-holding-company structures, and accelerate proprietary data and AI capabilities before the combined Omnicom-Acxiom ecosystem entrenches itself with enterprise clients.

eMarketer 2 min read

CTV Ad Spend Approaching $38B as Streaming Claims Record 47.5% of U.S. TV Viewing

Connected TV advertising is on track to reach $38 billion in 2026, growing ~14% year over year, with eMarketer projecting the channel will surpass traditional broadcast TV spending entirely by 2028. Nielsen's latest Gauge report found streaming now accounts for a record 47.5% of total U.S. television viewing time — up from roughly 38% two years ago — while broadcast TV without sports fell to just 9.8% of ad-supported viewership in Q4 2025. Critically for paid media planning: up to 50% of streaming advertisers are now moving CTV dollars from search and social budgets rather than from linear TV, meaning CTV is actively competing against your performance channels. Canadian agencies should note that Disney+'s absorption of Hulu creates the largest single combined ad-supported streaming audience, while Netflix's ad tier now exceeds 94 million monthly global users.

IAB: U.S. Ad Spend to Spike 9.5% in 2026 — But Hollywood Is at Risk of Being Left Behind

The IAB is forecasting U.S. total ad spend growth of 9.5% in 2026, driven by digital, AI-powered targeting, and the continued migration from linear television to streaming. WPP Media projects North American ad revenue — which hit $452.9 billion last year — will grow 7.6% in 2026, with Canada and Mexico getting a modest lift from FIFA World Cup advertising. The risk is concentrated in traditional television: WPP describes 2026 as an era of "creative destruction" where streaming cannibalizes linear, retail media captures legacy digital budgets, and AI answer engines reshape search. For Canadian paid media leaders, the FIFA World Cup co-hosting role in 2026 represents a once-in-a-generation premium inventory opportunity across both digital and broadcast — budget conversations with clients should be happening now.

49% of CTV Ads Are Still Traditional TV Spots — and Brands Are Leaving Performance on the Table

A new TripleLift study finds that nearly half of all CTV advertising still relies on repurposed 15- and 30-second linear TV spots — formats built for passive lean-back viewing that fail to exploit the interactive and data-rich environment of connected TV. Campaigns that combine traditional video with high-impact formats like pause ads, overlay units, and native streaming placements significantly outperform standard video alone on both brand recall and conversion metrics. This is a direct creative briefing challenge for agency teams: clients migrating linear TV budgets to CTV expect TV-equivalent production but need entirely new creative thinking to capture performance returns. Building a CTV-native creative capability — including interactive and shoppable formats — should be a priority pitch for Q2 client planning conversations.

Adobe + NVIDIA Partner to Build AI-Powered Creative Workflows for Marketing Teams

Adobe and NVIDIA announced a partnership to build new Firefly AI models and automated marketing workflows, using NVIDIA's GPU infrastructure to dramatically accelerate content creation, campaign variation testing, and asset adaptation across formats. The collaboration positions Adobe as the enterprise layer for AI-powered creative production, directly competing with standalone generative AI tools that have proliferated in marketing departments. Agencies should evaluate whether their creative tech stack is converging on interoperable platforms or creating fragmented AI debt that will slow production velocity.

Google Search Central 2 min read

Google Search Live Now Available Globally — Powered by Gemini 3.1 Flash Live Voice Model

Google announced that Search Live has launched globally across all languages and regions where AI Mode is available, driven by the new Gemini 3.1 Flash Live model designed for natural, conversational voice interactions. Search Live allows users to hold ongoing spoken conversations with Google Search — asking follow-up questions, getting real-time clarifications, and navigating results entirely by voice. The implication for earned media is significant: as voice-based AI search grows, traditional keyword optimization becomes insufficient, and structured content that answers multi-turn conversational queries becomes the new organic advantage. Agencies managing SEO for clients should begin auditing FAQ structures, schema markup, and conversational content depth to ensure discoverability within voice-driven AI search surfaces.

Search Engine Journal 2 min read

Zero-Click Search Expands Beyond Google — Now Dominating ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini & Meta AI

Zero-click search — where AI-generated answers satisfy queries without sending users to a website — has expanded well beyond Google's AI Overviews to become the default experience across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Bing Copilot, and Meta AI simultaneously. Research indicates that while zero-click surfaces reduce direct traffic, they significantly increase purchase intent and brand recall among users who do encounter a brand in AI answers. For paid media directors managing integrated digital programs, this creates an urgent question: how do you demonstrate organic value when traffic metrics no longer capture AI surface impressions? The answer lies in building GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) measurement frameworks alongside traditional SEO reporting — tracking citation mentions, AI answer inclusion rates, and assisted conversion attribution across AI search channels.

Meta Embeds Manus AI Agents Directly into Ads Manager — Autonomous Campaign Execution Is Here

Meta has integrated Manus AI — its autonomous agent technology acquired in late 2025 — directly into the Ads Manager navigation interface, giving advertisers access to an agent that can autonomously execute multi-step tasks including market research, report building, audience analysis, and campaign optimization without constant human hand-off. This is the practical realization of Meta's stated goal to fully automate advertising by end of 2026: brands supply creative assets and budget parameters, and the AI handles targeting, bidding, and iteration. For agency teams, the question is no longer whether to adopt AI-assisted campaign management but how to re-structure workflows and talent so that human expertise is applied to strategy and creative direction rather than mechanical execution. The agencies that figure this out earliest will achieve the efficiency gains that justify competitive fee structures.
