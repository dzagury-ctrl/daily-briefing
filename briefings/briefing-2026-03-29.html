Apple Maps Ads Are Coming to the US and Canada This Summer — With a Privacy-First Model

Apple officially announced paid search ads will launch on Apple Maps in the US and Canada this summer, with a dedicated portal opening April 14. Ads appear at the top of Maps search results and in a new "Suggested Places" experience, using an auction model where advertisers pay per tap or view — directly rivalling Google's local search ads. Critically, Apple's approach is built around on-device privacy: user location data is never associated with an Apple Account and never shared with advertisers, which will be a strong selling point to privacy-conscious Canadian consumers navigating stricter PIPEDA expectations. For Canadian agencies, this opens a brand-new local intent channel reaching iPhone's dominant iOS userbase — a significant opportunity for location-based retail, restaurant, and service clients ahead of the holiday planning season.

Omnicom Closes $13B IPG Acquisition — World's Largest Ad Holding Company, 4,000 Jobs Cut

Omnicom's landmark $13 billion acquisition of IPG is now complete, creating a marketing services giant managing over $70 billion in global media spend and combining revenues exceeding $25 billion. The merged entity is shuttering FCB, MullenLowe, and DDB, retaining only McCann, BBDO, and TBWA as global creative networks, and has set a target of $1.5 billion in annual savings — with $1 billion coming from labour reductions and roughly 4,000 positions eliminated. For independent and mid-size agencies like NP Digital, this consolidation creates opportunity: large holding company clients are increasingly frustrated with bureaucratic bloat, and 7 in 10 CMOs surveyed by Forrester expressed concern about the merger's impact on their business. Lean, performance-focused agencies that can move faster and prove ROI at every layer are well-positioned to pick up displaced clients.

TripleLift Study: 49% of CTV Ads Are Still Traditional TV Spots — A Massive Missed Opportunity

A new industry report from TripleLift reveals that nearly half of all CTV advertising still relies on standard 15- and 30-second linear TV spots designed for broadcast — creative formats that leave significant performance on the table on streaming. Google March 2026 Spam Update Targeted Thin AI Content and Parasitic SEO — Wrapped Last Week

Just ahead of the Core Update, Google completed its March 2026 Spam Update (March 24–25), specifically targeting thin AI-generated content, keyword stuffing, and parasitic SEO — where low-quality content is published on otherwise authoritative domains to piggyback their ranking strength. Search Engine Journal 3 min read

GEO Is the New SEO: Why Agencies Must Now Optimize for AI Citation, Not Just Google Rankings

In 2026, zero-click search has expanded far beyond Google — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Meta AI all now answer queries with sourced responses, meaning a brand's content becomes a reference rather than a destination. Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) — the practice of structuring content to be cited by AI systems — is rapidly becoming as important as traditional SEO. Google has also expanded AI Overviews globally and launched AI Mode with voice and camera search powered by Gemini 3.1 Flash Live. For paid media directors, this shift reframes the SEO-SEM budget relationship: as organic clicks decline under AI-powered answer engines, paid search becomes a more critical safety net — while GEO investment in high-authority, citable content protects long-term brand visibility at no incremental media cost.

OpenAI Launches Ads in ChatGPT — The AI Trust Contract Has Officially Shifted

OpenAI has begun testing contextual ads inside ChatGPT conversations, with ads triggered by discussion topics rather than browsing history, and users given opt-in/out controls. The rollout — following key hires including a chief revenue officer and ad lead — marks the moment generative AI platforms formally entered the advertising ecosystem. For agencies, this introduces a new inventory type that will require completely different creative formats, brand safety governance, and measurement approaches — while also validating the broader AI advertising trend that ChatGPT's 300M+ monthly active users represent an emerging paid media channel that can't be ignored.
