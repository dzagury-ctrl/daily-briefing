Google's Ad Tech Empire Faces Its Moment of Truth — Forced AdX Sale on the Table

Judge Brinkema's remedies ruling in United States v. Google is expected imminently — potentially forcing Google to divest its AdX ad exchange. The DOJ's core ask is structural separation: sell AdX, which currently charges publishers a 20% fee on transactions and gives Google an unfair advantage across its full buy-sell stack. If divestiture happens, programmatic economics will be reshuffled across every DSP and agency trading desk in North America. Canadian agencies running DV360 or Google Ads programmatic campaigns should closely monitor this, as a more competitive open exchange market would likely compress margins for Google but open alternatives like PubMatic, Magnite, and Index Exchange to significant upside.

Meta Aims to Fully Automate Advertising by End of 2026 — Brands Just Supply a Product Image

Meta has made it explicit: by end of 2026, advertisers may only need to provide a product image and a budget, and its AI will build, target, and optimize the entire campaign.
