Meta's Andromeda Has Rewritten the Rules of Paid Social — Creative Beats Audience Targeting Now

Meta's Andromeda ad retrieval system, which completed its global rollout in October 2025, has fundamentally reversed Meta's targeting logic: instead of starting with advertiser-defined audiences, it evaluates creative elements first — copy, format, visual content via computer vision, and audio — then finds the users most likely to engage. Meta reports Andromeda improved ad quality by 8% and recall by 6% across the platform; against a $196B ad revenue base, that's enormous. The practical implication for campaign managers is that tight audience segmentation is now less valuable than high-quality, differentiated creative — brands with strong creative output have a systematic advantage. Agencies should be restructuring their Meta workflow to prioritize creative production volume and A/B testing over audience architecture.
