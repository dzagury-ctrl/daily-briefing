FTC Settles with WPP, Publicis & Dentsu Over Brand Safety Collusion

The FTC and a coalition of eight states reached settlements with GroupM (WPP), Publicis, and Dentsu on April 15, alleging the holding companies unlawfully colluded since 2018 to impose uniform "brand safety" standards that systematically defunded certain publishers. Without admitting wrongdoing, all three firms agreed to stop using coordinated exclusion lists when placing media buys. For Canadian agency leads, this is a signal that governments are increasingly scrutinizing how brand safety tools are operationalized — and that blanket category-level blocks may carry legal and reputational risk. Client conversations about brand safety policies should now include documented rationale and a clear separation between editorial judgment and coordinated industry-level demonetization.

Meta Projected to Overtake Google in Global Ad Revenue for the First Time Ever

Meta is on track to generate $243.46 billion in global ad revenue in 2026, edging past Google's projected $239.54 billion — a historic first-ever inversion. This reflects Meta's AI-powered delivery improvements, Reels dominance in short-form video, and aggressive expansion of on-platform e-commerce tools across Instagram and WhatsApp. For a Director of Paid Media, this shifts the strategic center of gravity: default assumptions that Google anchors the upper funnel need stress-testing against Meta's growing full-funnel capabilities. Budget allocation models built before 2024 should be revisited with fresh attribution data before Q3 planning.

Announced April 21, the new Programmatic Governance Council convenes Dentsu, Omnicom, WPP, Amazon Ads, The Trade Desk, Disney, Magnite, PubMatic, and others to address transparency, fee disclosure, and AI governance across the $200B U.S. programmatic market. Google's AI Now Targets Bad Ads, Not Just Bad Advertisers — Silent Mid-Flight Disapprovals Rising

Google's latest enforcement data reveals a strategic shift: AI is blocking more individual policy-violating ads than ever before, but the number of full advertiser account suspensions is declining as the system surgically removes non-compliant placements rather than deactivating entire accounts. For agencies, this is a double-edged development — you're less likely to wake up to an account suspension, but individual ads can now be disapproved silently mid-campaign without triggering account-level alerts. The operational implication is clear: ad approval status monitoring needs to become a standing daily workflow for every active campaign, not a reactive process triggered by delivery anomalies or CPA spikes.

Microsoft Kills Free Prebid Cache in 7 Days — Publisher Video Ads at Risk of Silent Failure

On April 30 — one week from today — Microsoft will permanently shut down its free public Prebid Cache endpoint (prebid.adnxs.com/pbc), which currently underlies over 60% of configured publisher video ad setups. When the cache goes dark, publishers using Prebid with Google Ad Manager for video will experience silent ad failures: auctions will report wins, but no video will ever load — with no error messages to trace. Paid media directors need to immediately audit publisher partners in their programmatic mix for Prebid video dependency, and escalate urgently with any partner who hasn't migrated. SSPs including Magnite, PubMatic, and JW Player offer paid alternatives, but seven days is an extremely tight window — this deserves a call today, not a ticket next week.

Google AI Max for Search Now Universally Available — Reporting 14% Average Conversion Lift

Google made AI Max for Search campaigns available to all advertisers in April, enabling AI-driven text guidelines that automatically customize ad copy and expand keyword matching based on contextual intent signals. Google's own data reports a 14% average conversion lift, rising to 27% for accounts previously dependent on exact and phrase match keywords — a strong incentive to adopt. Bell Media Launches AdSyncs — Synchronized Visual Ads on In-Car Dashboards Across National FM Network

Bell Media has rolled out AdSyncs, a format that synchronizes branded text and logo displays on in-car dashboard screens with audio ads running simultaneously on FM stations including CHUM 104.5, Virgin Radio, Énergie, Rouge, and Pure Country. A Quu-commissioned study found the synchronized format increased purchase intent by 89%, brand favourability by 58%, and slogan recall by 57% — metrics that dwarf standard audio-only benchmarks. As Canadian commuter audiences remain one of the hardest-to-reach premium segments digitally, AdSyncs offers a measurable in-market moment format with real creative differentiation. For media planners in automotive, QSR, retail, and financial categories, this format warrants a test-and-learn allocation in H2 2026 briefs.
