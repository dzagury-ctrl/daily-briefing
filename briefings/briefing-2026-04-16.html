Meta Overtakes Google in Global Ad Revenue for the First Time Ever

eMarketer now projects Meta will generate $243.46B in net worldwide ad revenues in 2026, edging past Google's $239.54B — a historic first. Meta's growth is accelerating to 24.1% YoY while Google's holds at 11.9%, driven by AI-powered tools like Advantage+, Reels monetization, and automated creative. For a Director of Paid Media, this signals a fundamental rebalancing of budget allocation: Meta's AI stack is delivering measurable conversion lifts that are difficult to ignore. Expect clients to ask hard questions about whether their Google-heavy mix still reflects where audiences and returns actually are.

CMO Ian Colley, Head of Comms Melinda Zurich, and SVP of Ventura Matthew Henick have all departed The Trade Desk simultaneously — the latest in a string of C-suite exits that now includes its former CFO and three board members. Google has introduced a new shopping ad format that places labeled "Sponsored" retailer listings directly within AI Mode conversation results — not just alongside them. Start monitoring impression share in AI Mode placements now.

Search Engine Land / Search Engine Journal 3 min read

Google March 2026 Core Update Complete: News Sites See Modest Gains, Thin Content Hit Hard

The March 2026 core update — which started March 27 and completed rollout on April 8 — placed a clear premium on first-hand expertise and genuine topic depth. News sites saw modest gains, while sites with thin or aggregated coverage dropped significantly in organic visibility. Critically, core updates are comparative: pages can lose rankings even when nothing on-page changed, simply because a competitor's content now better satisfies searcher intent. For teams managing SEO alongside paid search, this is a reminder that organic volatility directly affects overall cost efficiency — when organic drops, CPCs often rise as you're forced to cover lost traffic with paid spend. Now is the time to audit any organic positions your clients depend on.

Search Engine Land 3 min read

Google Retiring Dynamic Search Ads in September — All Eligible Campaigns Upgrade to AI Max

Google has confirmed that Dynamic Search Ads (DSA), along with automatically created assets and broad match campaigns, will be automatically upgraded to AI Max for Search in September 2026. AI Max is already universally available, with Google reporting a typical 14% lift in conversions or conversion value at similar CPA/ROAS for advertisers who activate it. The forced migration is significant: DSA has been a long-standing workhorse for e-commerce and catalog-heavy advertisers, and the transition to AI Max changes how headline generation, URL expansion, and targeting work at a fundamental level. Accounts should be audited and opted in proactively now to maintain control — rather than waiting for Google to flip the switch in September.

IAB Canada Flags Tariff Headwinds for Canadian Digital Advertising Ecosystem

IAB Canada has formally weighed in on the U.S.-Canada trade war's implications for the Canadian digital advertising ecosystem, warning that tariffs place upward pressure on advertiser costs and could compress marketing budgets as brands face higher goods pricing and economic uncertainty. For agency teams, this is a dual-signal environment: some verticals (retail, CPG, automotive) may pull back ad spend in response to margin pressure, while government and "Buy Canadian" brand campaigns represent near-term new business and incremental media activation opportunities.
