ChatGPT Ads Hit $100M Annualized Revenue in Six Weeks — Self-Serve Opens in April

OpenAI's ad pilot, launched February 9 for logged-in U.S. adults, crossed $100M in annualized recurring revenue within six weeks — with 600+ active advertisers and no measurable impact on privacy trust metrics. The company has tapped David Dugan (ex-Meta) to lead global ad solutions and is now opening self-serve access this month, with early expansion tests underway in Canada, Australia, and New Zealand. For Canadian paid media directors, this is the earliest signal that ChatGPT will become a legitimate bidding environment within months — and the self-serve rollout means you should be preparing audience, creative, and measurement frameworks now, not at launch. The platform's ad placement (below AI responses, clearly labeled, non-influencing) mirrors a native/contextual model that will require rethinking traditional search budgeting playbooks.

Search Engine Journal 3 min read Happening Now

Google AI Max for Search Campaigns Now Universally Available — 14–27% Conversion Lift Reported

After a phased beta rollout through Q1, Google's AI Max for Search is now open to all advertisers as of April 2026. The feature expands keyword matching using AI, dynamically rewrites ad copy within brand-approved guardrails, and automates audience targeting — with Google citing a 14% average conversion lift at similar CPA, jumping to 27% for accounts still reliant on exact/phrase match. The text guidelines feature (brand tone, language restrictions, phrase exclusions) is the critical addition for agency accounts where client brand safety is non-negotiable. Directors should audit their current match-type strategy now: the accounts most exposed to disruption are those with tightly controlled keyword lists that haven't yet tested broad match or Performance Max. This is effectively Google compelling the industry to cede more control to its AI — plan your testing framework before clients ask why CPAs shifted.

Financial Content 3 min read

Google's March 2026 Core Update: 71% Traffic Drop for AI-Generated Sites, 22% Gain for Original Research

Analysis of 600,000 web pages following Google's March 2026 Core Update — which wrapped in 45 days — reveals a stark bifurcation: mass-produced AI content suffered 71% organic traffic drops, while sites publishing original data-backed content gained 22% visibility. Critically, researchers found near-zero correlation between AI use and ranking penalties, meaning the signal is quality and expertise, not the tool used to produce content. For agency SEO teams and the clients they manage, this update functionally closes the door on bulk AI content strategies that lack first-party data or genuine subject matter expertise. Any client campaigns still relying on AI-spun blog volume need an immediate content audit — and the 22% winner category is an actionable playbook: invest in original studies, proprietary data, and named expert contributions.

NewFronts 2026: Samsung and Amazon DSP Unite to Bring "Add to Cart" Directly to Your TV Remote

At NewFronts this week, Samsung TV Plus announced a deep integration with Amazon DSP that makes it the first external CTV platform to support Amazon's interactive video ad tech — launching July 2026. Viewers on Samsung smart TVs can now add products directly to their Amazon cart, "Send to Phone" a link, or sign up for services, all via remote without leaving the content. Canadian media directors should note this won't be available in Canada at launch (US rollout first), but it signals where the industry is heading and should factor into upfront planning discussions with retail clients who are currently over-indexed on search and social.

CTV Now Cannibalizing Search and Social Budgets — Roku Forecasts 50% of New Spend Shifting From Digital

New data from eMarketer projects 14% CTV ad spend growth in 2026, with nearly 70% of CTV advertisers planning budget increases — averaging 17% more. Roku's forecast is the headline: up to 50% of new CTV investment will be funded by budgets pulled from search and social, not from incremental spend. Simultaneously, ad-supported content now represents 74% of all TV viewing, and streaming holds 47.5% of total U.S. television watch time. For paid media directors managing channel mix, the practical implication is that CTV justification is getting easier — but the zero-sum dynamic means search and social teams should prepare for internal budget pressure. Build CTV measurement frameworks that satisfy the same performance accountability clients expect from Google and Meta, or those conversations will go poorly.

Vaseline's Real-Time TikTok Strategy: Social Listening Replaces Scheduled Content

Unilever's Vaseline has fundamentally restructured its content model around social listening — monitoring TikTok communities for organic product usage hacks, then responding with expert-validated content that amplifies those same trends. Rather than scripted influencer campaigns, the brand acts as a reactive authority within conversations already happening. The underlying infrastructure requirement is a real-time social intelligence tool and a content approval process fast enough to capitalize on 24–48 hour trend windows — neither of which most agencies have optimized.

ALM Corp 3 min read

AI Overviews Now on 13% of All Google Queries — Click-Through Rates on Listings Below Drop 42%

New industry data confirms that when Google's AI Overviews appear on a SERP, click-through rates for the organic listings beneath them fall by approximately 42%. AI Overviews now trigger on roughly 13% of all queries, with coverage concentrated in informational, how-to, and research-intent searches. The CTR compression isn't uniform — navigational queries and high-commercial-intent searches are largely unaffected — but for clients whose SEO strategies rely on informational content to drive upper-funnel traffic, this is a structural revenue threat. Paid search teams should be monitoring whether AI Overview prevalence is displacing clicks on branded or competitor terms, and SEO teams need to shift KPIs from raw organic impressions to engagement-adjusted metrics that account for zero-click queries. This is also an argument for investing more in featured snippet and AI Overview source optimization.

Google's New E-E-A-T Signal: Author Bios and "About" Pages Now Measurably Impact Rankings

Following the March 2026 core update, SEO practitioners are confirming that Google's trust signals now include structured author attribution — specifically, named experts with verifiable credentials, linked author bio pages, and comprehensive "About Us" content. Sites with anonymous or generic authorship are underperforming against structurally identical content from named contributors. Client industries with natural subject matter expertise (finance, health, legal, home services) have the largest upside from building proper author infrastructure, and the ROI case is becoming easier to quantify as ranking correlations strengthen.

Meta's Full Ad Automation Vision: Submit a URL, Get a Live Campaign — The Agency Threat Is Real

Meta is now rolling out end-to-end AI-automated campaigns where advertisers supply only a business URL and a budget — the AI generates creative (images, video, copy), selects audiences, allocates across Facebook and Instagram, and optimizes bids. The market reacted bluntly: on the day of the announcement, Interpublic dropped 1.9%, Omnicom fell 3.2%, Publicis slid 3.8%, and WPP declined 2.2%. For agency leaders, the honest read is that Meta is automating the execution layer of paid social — the commodity work. The sustainable agency value proposition shifts decisively toward strategy, brand voice governance, cross-channel orchestration, and the measurement frameworks that Meta's black-box AI cannot provide. Directors should be documenting — explicitly — what their team contributes beyond the tasks Meta is automating.
