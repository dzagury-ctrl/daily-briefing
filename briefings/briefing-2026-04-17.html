IAB / PwC 3 min read Happening Now

Digital Ad Revenue Hits $294.6B in 2025 — Social Overtakes Search for First Time

The IAB/PwC Internet Advertising Revenue Report (released yesterday) confirmed that 2025 was a record-breaking year for digital advertising — $294.6B, up 13.9% YoY — even without the boost of elections, the Olympics, or FIFA. Social media edged past search for the first time, posting $117.7B (+32.6%) versus search's $114.2B (+11%), powered by creator economy integrations and commerce-native formats. For a Director of Paid Media, the signal is clear: budget pressure to shift toward social performance channels will intensify heading into 2026 planning cycles. The programmatic surge (+20.5% to $162.4B) also validates an automation-first posture for media buying.

Digiday 4 min read Happening Now

Google AdX Antitrust Ruling Overdue — Forced Sale of Ad Exchange Still on the Table

Judge Leonie Brinkema's self-imposed March 31 deadline for her AdX remedies ruling has passed with no public filing, and legal observers say the decision is imminent. The DOJ is pushing for a structural breakup — forcing Google to divest both AdX and DoubleClick for Publishers (DFP) — while Google argues behavioral concessions are sufficient. If the DOJ prevails, the open-web programmatic ecosystem would be fundamentally restructured: open auction dynamics, transparent bid data, and third-party exchange competition would reshape CPMs and inventory access across every agency's buy. Canadian agencies running programmatic display should be preparing scenario plans now.

A new Frost & Sullivan strategic imperatives report (released yesterday) frames 2026 as the inflection point where AI transitions from a feature to the operational backbone of advertising — they're calling it the "Agentic Economy." For Canadian agencies, the implication is that mid-tier and boutique shops face a window of 12–18 months to retool workflows before AI-native competitors erode their positioning on efficiency grounds. The firms that define their human value-add now will be better positioned to resist commoditization.

Omnicom Retires IPG Mediabrands — UM, Initiative & Mediahub Folded Into Single Media Platform

Following the November 2025 close of its $9B acquisition of IPG, Omnicom has now retired the IPG Mediabrands brand entirely, collapsing UM, Initiative, and Mediahub into a unified Omnicom Media platform. The newly combined entity is the world's largest holding company with ~$25B in combined revenue, and the restructuring has eliminated an estimated 4,000 positions globally. For independent and mid-size agencies like NP Digital, this consolidation creates a client acquisition opportunity: brands caught in restructuring uncertainty or assigned to unfamiliar new teams will be actively evaluating alternatives. The window is open to position agile specialists as a counterpoint to mega-holding-company scale.

Meta's AI Ad Automation Push: Brands Supply a Budget and a Photo — AI Handles Everything Else

Meta is accelerating its vision for fully automated advertising: a system where brands provide only a product image and a budget cap, and the AI generates creatives, selects placements (Facebook vs. Instagram), and optimizes in real time — with no manual trafficking. Meta's Advantage+ campaigns already account for 60–70% of some agencies' Meta spending, but the new direction would further erode creative control and transparency. For NP Digital, this is a positioning moment — the agencies that can bring differentiating creative strategy on top of AI efficiency will command premium fees as commoditized clients race to the bottom.

Google's March 2026 Core Update: AI Content Takes a 71% Traffic Hit, Volatility Highest in Years

Google's March 2026 broad core update — the first of the year — finished rolling out after 12 days, and its impact was severe: analysis of 600,000 web pages showed mass-produced AI content sites lost up to 71% of their traffic, with affiliate marketing verticals hit hardest. Nearly 80% of top-three SERP positions shifted, and one in four top-10 pages fell completely out of the top 100. The update reinforces a consistent signal: Google is systematically penalizing velocity-over-quality AI publishing strategies, while rewarding sites with proprietary data, strong brand signals, and genuine topical authority. For SEO teams servicing clients across any content-heavy vertical, this is a mandate to audit AI content ratios and build differentiated owned data assets immediately.

Search Engine Journal 2 min read

Google Search Console Impression Bug Fixed After 11-Month Over-Reporting Error

Google has confirmed a Search Console logging bug that caused impression counts to be inflated beginning May 13, 2025 — meaning roughly 11 months of GSC impression data may misrepresent true organic visibility for many sites. Historical performance comparisons built on that data window are now unreliable, and any CTR benchmarking tied to those figures needs to be recalibrated. For paid media directors overseeing integrated search programs, this is worth flagging immediately with SEO colleagues: bid strategy decisions informed by organic impression trends from the affected period may need revisiting. Google has corrected the logging going forward, but historical data has not been retroactively fixed.

HubSpot Pivots to Outcome-Based AI Pricing: $0.50 Per Resolved Conversation, $1 Per Qualified Lead

HubSpot restructured pricing for its Breeze AI agents this month, moving from flat monthly fees to pure outcome-based billing: $0.50 per resolved customer service conversation and $1 per qualified lead generated by the Prospecting Agent. HubSpot reports the Customer Agent resolves 65% of conversations and cuts resolution time by 39% — making it a compelling ROI proposition for clients at scale. This is a signal of where the broader martech market is heading: AI tools that can't demonstrate clear per-outcome economics will struggle to justify renewal in budget cycles. For agency recommendations, this model makes it easier to quantify AI ROI for clients skeptical of flat SaaS fees, and it's a pricing conversation to start having now.
