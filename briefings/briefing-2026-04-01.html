Federal Judge Rules Google Holds Illegal Ad Tech Monopoly — DOJ Pushes for AdX Sale

A federal judge has confirmed that Google maintained an illegal monopoly across two ad tech product markets, and the U.S. Department of Justice is now pursuing structural remedies including a forced divestiture of Google's ad exchange, AdX. Google has separately offered product concessions in Europe to avoid an EU-mandated sell-off, but U.S. regulators appear less willing to accept half-measures. If AdX is spun off, the entire programmatic supply chain — publisher floors, auction dynamics, fee structures — would be reset, potentially opening arbitrage windows for agile buyers. Canadian agencies should track this closely: any structural break in the Google ad stack will ripple into how Performance Max, DV360, and SA360 interoperate, directly affecting campaign architecture decisions.

Leo Burnett UK's "Camera Rolls" campaign for McDonald's UK built a brand platform out of a universal behavioural truth: the last frame in almost any night out is a McDonald's bag.
